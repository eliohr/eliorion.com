CHAPTER I: THE LONG RUN
ELI ORION
Multi-instrumentalist, composer, and producer Eli Orion Hooker Reese (local to Minneapolis, MN, and in Appleton, WI) explores a diverse palette of sonic possibilities. There's a lot to listen for in his recordings—lush string sections, blown-out 808s, fingerstyle guitar… Eli is both humorously sardonic and intimately sincere, with lyrics often introspective but sometimes plainly fun. Inspirations include underscores, Bon Iver, The Microphones, and gabby start.

Eli Orion is releasing a pop rock album. Three singles are available now. Three chapters and a fourth single lead up to the album's release on May 17, 2024. “Everything You Didn’t Want to Hear” is a 13-track journey from naivety, through vain self-deceit, and out from the sludge into truth. It is also a collection of all the songs Eli’s had the most fun making yet.

The Long Run contains three songs from the first third of Eli Orion’s upcoming album, “Everything you Didn’t Want to Hear.” High Spirits and The Bells were the first songs written for the album, and have been through many evolutions. High Spirits introduces the album’s energetic, playful nature within the world of pop rock, as well as its surprise ventures through pop-punk, alt-pop, and heavy programmed drums. Eli has a lot to lose. In The Bells, he reaches for something already gone. This one leans into shifted vocals, huge dynamic ranges, electronic sounds, and the beloved underscores pop-punk style. The sun sets into Who Needs? Eli is now angry and bargaining in his grief. It gets worse before it gets better—in April, Chapter II follows the next part of this story. But for now, here’s Chapter I: The Long Run, out March 22, 2024.
