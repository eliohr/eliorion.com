EVERYTHING YOU DIDN'T WANT TO HEAR
ELI ORION
Multi-instrumentalist, composer, and producer Eli Orion Hooker Reese explores a diverse palette of sonic possibilities. There's a lot to listen for in his recordings—lush string sections, blown-out 808s, fingerstyle guitar…

Eli asks a lot of questions. He is both humorously sardonic and intimately sincere, with lyrics often introspective but sometimes plainly fun. Influences and inspirations include underscores, Bon Iver, The Microphones, and gabby start.

Four singles and three chapters lead up to this album's release. “Everything You Didn’t Want to Hear” is a 13-track journey from naivety, through vain self-deceit, and out from the sludge into truth. It is also a collection of all the songs Eli’s had the most fun making yet.

This album narrates the way my mindset has shifted over the last few years: teenage naivety (“weed and fast food whenever you want!”), nihilism (infidelity, emotional manipulation, breakup), and existentialism (“hey let’s all find the beauty in life and each other”). In Chapter I, tracks like "High Spirits" and "The Bells" introduce the album’s playful essence, blending pop-rock and pop-punk influences with alt-pop elements (underscores, gabby start). Chapter II, featuring "Rebound Baby,” explores 80s pop influences, The 1975's aesthetic, and more traditional pop sounds. Chapter III emerges from the sludge in the sonic worlds of folk guitar, autotune, jazz harmony, memories, Bon Iver, and dance synths. The album's title track is still reaching for something but is willing to embrace life's absurdity with contemporary y2k pop-rock flair, bringing the journey full circle.
