INFINITY (RIGHT TRACK, PT. 2)
ELI ORION
Multi-instrumentalist, composer, and producer Eli Orion Hooker Reese (local to Minneapolis, MN, and in Appleton, WI) explores a diverse palette of sonic possibilities. There's a lot to listen for in his recordings—lush string sections, blown-out 808s, fingerstyle guitar… Eli is both humorously sardonic and intimately sincere, with lyrics often introspective but sometimes plainly fun. Inspirations include underscores, Bon Iver, The Microphones, and gabby start.

Eli Orion is releasing a pop rock album. Four singles and three chapters, chronologically populating every two weeks, lead up to the album's release on May 17, 2024. “Everything You Didn’t Want to Hear” is a 13-track journey from naivety, through vain self-deceit, and out from the sludge into truth. It is also a collection of all the songs Eli’s had the most fun making yet.

Infinity is the last single from the album. The word “hyperpop” has been gentrified so this song can be classified as “fun and loud”. References include gabby start, Porter Robinson, underscores, and Owl City. As of writing this, a music video is in production, and will be finished by the end of March. The video and song employ camp to deconstruct the meaning of life with reckless abandon. The electronic drums are like Travis Barker if he was a mouse on ritalin and everything else is packed with playful ear candy. It’s all just fun and games anyway!
