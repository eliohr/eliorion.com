I QUIT EVERYTHING
ELI ORION
Multi-instrumentalist, composer, and producer Eli Orion Hooker Reese (local to Minneapolis, MN, and Appleton, WI) explores a diverse palette of sonic possibilities. There's a lot to listen for in his recordings—lush string sections, blown-out 808s, fingerstyle guitar… Eli asks a lot of questions. He is both humorously sardonic and intimately sincere, with lyrics often introspective but sometimes plainly fun. Influences and inspirations include underscores, Big Thief, Bon Iver, The Microphones, and gabby start.

Eli Orion has a pop rock album: Everything You Didn’t Want to Hear. This track, set for July 31st, serves as an epilogue.

“Watch as I calcify; think I’ll take medicine for the rest of my life…” I’m not a kid anymore and it’s scary to experience my mind, body, and soul solidifying. Not that literally everyone else hasn’t experienced the exact same feeling. But I guess that’s why I’m talking about it.

Drawing from my roots in the American fiddle tradition and love for electronic sounds, this song and the accompanying video capture a vignette of some time at my family home in December of 2023. Whether it’s about letting go of harmful habits or the stress of personal expectations (developing a fulfilling career, releasing high-quality music, maintaining meaningful relationships, etc.), laying down all baggage to take steps in a new direction appeals to me, especially in moments when everything’s so incredibly heavy. Even the title nods to the end of the age of “Everything”.
